Priority Review

Separate renewal-critical items from longer-term risk management initiatives.

The immediate question is not whether every possible coverage needs to be purchased before renewal. The immediate question is which items materially affect the renewal decision, which items need executive awareness, and which items need to become part of the post-renewal plan.

Lane 1

Renewal-Critical

  • Review GL and excess structure.
  • Review property, business income, and equipment breakdown terms.
  • Review product recall wording and recall triggers.
  • Evaluate auto, umbrella, D&O, EPLI, crime, and social engineering terms.
  • Review Stock Throughput / Cargo for inventory in transit, 3PLs, and warehoused goods.
  • Compare quotes by coverage quality, not only premium.
Lane 2

Strategic Gap Decisions

  • Cyber Liability for e-commerce, ransomware, data, PCI, and downtime risk.
  • Media / Content Liability for founder-led content, testimonials, advertising, and product claims.
  • Manufacturer’s E&O if formulation, labeling/spec errors, private label, or advisory exposure exists.
  • Key person and executive protection items tied to brand dependency.
Lane 3

Trigger-Based

  • Clinical trials, if product testing or trial activity is contemplated.
  • Event liability for launches, pop-ups, appearances, sampling, and conferences.
  • IP defense / enforcement for brand names, trade dress, packaging, content, and formulas.
  • Trade credit for material distributor, retailer, or international receivable exposure.
  • Foreign package / political risk if international sales, travel, or sourcing becomes material.
Risk Lens

The largest exposures are multi-policy scenarios.

For a growth-stage consumer wellness brand, the material claim scenarios rarely stay inside one policy. The renewal review will test how the program responds when several coverage lines are implicated at once.

Product Incident

Potentially implicates products liability, recall, business income, crisis communications, retailer chargebacks, and brand rehabilitation.

Digital / E-Commerce Disruption

Potentially implicates cyber, business interruption, dependent vendor outage, payment risk, customer data, and reputation harm.

Advertising / Influencer Claim

Potentially implicates media liability, advertising injury, FTC-sensitive content, IP infringement, testimonials, and product performance claims.

Post-Renewal Roadmap

Turn the renewal into a broader risk management plan.

The broader risk work will not be forced into the current renewal window. Instead, SIP will use the renewal as the starting point for a practical post-renewal cadence with Just Ingredients’ leadership team.

First 30 Days

Executive Alignment & Data Intake

Establish the risk management agenda and gather the information needed to evaluate coverage adequacy beyond the renewal.

  • Product and revenue breakdown by category
  • Distribution channel mix
  • Manufacturing, co-packer, 3PL, and supplier map
  • Claims, complaints, and adverse event history
Days 31–60

Coverage & Contract Review

Review the program through real claim scenarios, contract obligations, and operational dependencies.

  • Policy wording, exclusions, and sublimits
  • Retailer, supplier, co-packer, and 3PL insurance requirements
  • Recall plan and crisis communications readiness
  • BI, contingent BI, inventory, and supply chain dependencies
Days 61–90

Risk Strategy Presentation

Present leadership with a prioritized risk plan that separates insurance placement, operational mitigation, and executive decisions.

  • Cyber and media liability strategy
  • Product recall and product liability structure
  • Stock throughput / cargo and inventory risk
  • Key person, executive protection, and enterprise value considerations
Ongoing Cadence

Recommended leadership rhythm.

Quarterly Risk Check-In

Review new product categories, distribution changes, contracts, incidents, claims, and operational shifts.

Mid-Year Coverage Audit

Revisit coverage structure, gap items, and any risk controls that could improve the next renewal submission.

Renewal Readiness Meeting

Start the next renewal with a stronger underwriting narrative, cleaner data, and better-defined priorities.
